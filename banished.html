Banished

Print, Motion;
Client: RIAM Opera

Banished is an opera about women prisoners
on ships in the 1800s. My poster focused on
the vast number of women who were imprisoned
on these ships and transported to Australia.
This is graphically represented through fingerprints,
as it was how prisoners were documented at the
time and it is also the symbol of identity, which is
something that was taken from the female prisoners.
The silver vinyl represents their lives being tarnished
by this experience and also alludes to grubby fingers
on prison bars. The booklet format also reflects the
idea of multiples by use of tabs for section openings.
